01. Semantic Fingerprinting

Every writer has a "Semantic Fingerprint"—a specific way they use rhythm, vocabulary, and sentence length to convey authority. To clone a voice, you cannot just say "write like [Name]." You must analyze the Burstiness (rhythm variation) and Perplexity (complexity) of their prose. A Prompt Architect extracts these metrics and hard-codes them as instructional parameters within the vault.

The Voice Cloning Stack

To achieve a perfect 1:1 clone, your architecture must include:

  • Lexical Preferences: A "whitelist" of power words the brand uses and a "blacklist" of corporate fluff they avoid.
  • Structural Logic: Instructions on sentence cadence—e.g., "Always lead with a short, punchy sentence followed by a complex explanation."
  • Emotional Mapping: Defining the level of skepticism, optimism, or industrial grit the brand projects.
